This is Techpreneur

We are a global tech education nonprofit that empowers students to become leaders, creators and problem-solvers in their society.


Our Techpreneur program equips young Students in secondary and tertiary institutions to become tech entrepreneurs and leaders. With the support of volunteer mentors and parents, students work in teams to code mobile apps that address real-world problems.


A collaborative approach


Our learning model


We use a three-part model to help students develop greater self-efficacy and change their attitudes towards STEM.



1. Identify real-world problems: Participants find a problem in their community and develop a solution to it, which helps bring technology to life in an immediate, tangible way.



2. Build a team: Participants work in teams—students work with a group of friends supported by a mentor (and a parent, if the student is younger than 12) and connected to a local Chapter or Club Ambassador. Together they brainstorm, share the workload, and cheer each other on. When everyone's contributing, everyone is also building a long-term community of learning.



3. Get the Community Involved: We dedicate time to building trust with the local community leaders who bring Techpreneur to their schools, organizations, or towns. Techpreneur programs are designed to be flexible, so they can align with diverse community needs and goals and plug in to existing support networks.

OUR
GOAL

Our goal is to boost creativity among students and youths, making them innovative, promote S.T.E.M Education and to create a business ecosystem for young innovators.


Our objectives are as follows;


  1. Promotion of S.T.E.M Education.
  2. To increase Student presentation Skill and prepare them to become greater Leaders in their Society.
  3. To build creative minds, problem solvers and critical thinkers through a technopreneurship program.
  4. To actively encourage and bridge the gap of Students in technology by 10% each year.
  5. To encourage students in technology into local content research.

We’re still learning and growing, and we hope you’ll join us.
